The studio

One team for the whole of a space.

Liag is an architecture and interior design studio in India. We design buildings and the lives inside them — and we believe those two things should never be drawn by different hands.

Who we are

A practice built around a single, stubborn idea.

Most projects pass through many hands. An architect draws the building, then walks away. An interior designer inherits the result and works around it. A stylist arrives last to dress whatever's left. At every handover, a little of the original intent leaks out.

We started Liag in 2009 to close those gaps. One team carries a project from the first structural sketch to the last cushion placed — so the window sits where the light is needed, and the wall is where the sofa wants to be.

Today we're a studio of twelve, working across homes, workplaces, and cultural spaces. We've grown, but the idea hasn't changed.

What we believe

Three things we won't compromise.

01

One studio, no handoffs

The same team designs your building and its interior. Nothing gets lost in translation between disciplines because there is no translation — it's one conversation.

02

The site leads

We don't arrive with a house style to impose. Every project starts with its site, its light, and the people who'll use it — the design follows from there.

03

Built to last

Restraint over trend. We choose materials and details that age well, so a space still feels considered a decade after we hand over the keys.
