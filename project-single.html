The Riverside House

A family home designed from the ground up — structure and interior drawn together so the light, the rooms, and the life inside all belong to one idea.

The brief

One family, one river view, and a plot that sloped the wrong way.

The clients came to us with a steep riverside plot and a simple ask: a home that felt open to the water without feeling exposed to the road behind it. The challenge was the slope — the best views sat where the ground fell away fastest.

Rather than fight the site, we let it lead. The structure steps down with the land, so every main room opens onto the river while the entry and service spaces tuck into the hillside. Because our team designed both the building and the interior, the window lines were set to frame the water exactly where the sofa, the dining table, and the bed would later sit.

Inside, a restrained material palette — warm timber, lime plaster, and brushed brass — keeps the focus on the changing light off the river. Built-in joinery does the heavy lifting, so the rooms stay calm and uncluttered. The result is a home that reads as a single, continuous idea from the foundation to the last styled shelf.

“

They didn't hand us a building and leave us to furnish it. The same people who drew the walls placed the furniture. It shows in every room — nothing feels like an afterthought.

AR Ayesha Rahman Homeowner · The Riverside House
